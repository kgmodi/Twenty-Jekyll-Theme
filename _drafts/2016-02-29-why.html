Why the trip?


The most elementary and vital question was why do we want to do this trip?I should have started of the blog with this.But honestly these thought were more preeminent as we thought more of the trip. 

It appeared crazy and meaningless to some.Quitting jobs, wasting money, unnecessary exposing one selves to dangers were the few of varied responses we got from people.

But in our heads and hearts we knew why we wanted to do the journey.

Kunal:
The driving force  for Kunal behind doing the journey was to experience the excitement of crossing borders and reaching the homeland. He wanted to mimic the silk route.The idea of driving across the countries was fascinating to Kunal. Study,get a job,get married,have kids,buy a home,pay mortgages,get old, retire was not the only thing he was looking at."I want to do something different in my life." "I want to know the meaning and purpose of life. I know I want to do something to help some one."
"On my previous trip 'Timbuktu challenge' London to Mali I learned a lot, I could not finish the last lap due to visa issues, and that is when I decided to drive to India."
Going for a charity mission for a adventure seems the way to go. "Its like running a marathon for a cause." Its like clubbing your passion to be a explorer with the idea of doing good. 

Shrida:
Sole purpose and mission of Shrida was to raise awareness about a good cause.She mentioned that as she drives across miles,talking to hundreds of people about her project and mission,she would be sowing a seed of goodness in that individuals mind. "Even if that person will not donate or contribute to my charity he will feel like contributing it towards some other charity." "Its like working off field for my project." Its about raising awareness about a mission. Shrida has been an active member in raising awareness, fund raising for lot of projects including but not limited to the cancer research institute.
