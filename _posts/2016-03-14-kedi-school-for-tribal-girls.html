Education for Tribal Girls


A family friend Suryakant Shah introduced us to a couple Aparna and Pankaj Kadikar, who presently live in Dharampur, Valsad, Gujarat India. This couple of Indian origin lived in Los Angeles, US and worked as Architect and Investment banker. At 45 yrs of their age they decided to move back to India. They opened a residential school for tribal girls in a small district of Gujarat, India. 
The problems faced for girl education are:
1. Lack of schools: The educational facilities available are very scarce, offering negligible quality education and existing ones are located in distant areas. Hostel facilities are rare in those places.
2. Gender inequality: Even today the girl child does not get the same importance and emphasis to education in tribal communities of Dharampur and Dang districts due to social reasons.
3. Poverty and Deprivation: Lots of families are under line of poverty. This forces the girl child to child labour and taking care of siblings. The essential health care, living conditions and food necessities are barely met, spending money for education of girl child is an unnecessary expense.

This couple open up a school with residential facilities free of cost for this girls for grade 8th to grade 12th. The focus of education was quality of education and holistic development of this girls. Along with academic education, vocational training, environmental education and hygiene and sanitation training is imparted to this girls. Education and training is driven to make them self independent and economically stable.

The initial few years Pankaj and Aparna were full of challenges. They went door to door convincing parents in the tribal district to send their girls to Kedi School. This is the 10th year of school now. The school is running successfully as the community has seen the difference in their daughters and overall progress in their families. There are around 100 girls presently in the school with a staff of 10-12 teachers. Few being on honorary basis.

Kunal, Shrida and  I really liked the cause. We were really impressed by Aparna and Pankaj's work. The change they are bringing about by working at root level is really commendable. I thought to myself, may be I will like to do something similar in my later life.

Kedi school is coming up with new project for scholarship programs for girls after 12th grade. We decided to raise awareness and funds for the same.
