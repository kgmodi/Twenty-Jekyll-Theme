Hospice Center


Shrida introduced us to Skanda Vale. She has been very closely associated with the Skanda Vale Hospice in Wales, U.K and  has been running 10 K since the last five years to raise funds for the same institute. 
Skanda Vale is a project run by monks and nuns of Skanda Vale Monastery. The project began in 1988 offering home care services to terminally ill patients and to people having life limiting conditions. The purpose was to help patients deal with the symptoms, pain and suffering of their terminal days and help them live well. 
Hospice is not about a doom and gloom condition. It is not necessarily about sick or ailing people in bed surrounded by a loud television. Skanda Vale is more about creating a home away from home with a warm, friendly, relaxed atmosphere. Skanda Vale is about the people finding optimism and a sense of normality (and even fun) during a very challenging time of life. Skanda Vale believes in 'We care' apart from just providing medical treatment to patients. It is to provide holistic care and help practically, emotionally, socially and spiritually. It helps patients to cope with the anxiety of death and dying and also supports their families during the same phase.
There is an inpatient center and also a day respite care service where people with Cancer, breathing difficulties, Multiple Sclerosis, Lung problems and any Chronic pain can come and visit the center. People who know they are going to die soon may be obliged to put a brave face at home to minimize the emotional effects on their family. When patients visit this center they can be themselves and share their pain and mental state of mind with others in a similar state. 
There are various services offered including large lounge, dining area and conservatory, home cooked meals meeting diet specific requirements, hydraulic bath and chair lifts for assisted bathing, reflexology, massage and aromatherapy, art and craft workshops, counselling services, spiritual training and beautiful gardens with country side views.
Physical Therapy and Occupational Therapy are incorporated in the rehabilitative care of the inpatients. 

In 2003 Skanda Vale opened in Llandysul, UK. The location is on the border of Pembrokeshire, Carmarthenshire and Ceredigion and thus easily accessible to the rural community. The projects to develop this hospice have been a long process. All services and even inpatient respite are free of cost to patients. There are no NHS or government grants. All projects are run on donations and fund raising events. 

We realized we would love to be instrumental by our journey to  raise awareness for Skanda Vale.
