Route, Re-Route, Re-Re-Route, DeRoute!



We started filling up multiple spread sheets deciding our route. Every new development, research and piece of information started changing our way to Mumbai.
The starting point is fixed (New York) and final destination is fixed (Mumbai) the rest is to be decided. The plan is to fly from New York To London and then start driving from London across land borders to Mumbai.
The safety, uncertainty of borders, cost, visa requirements, knowledge of road conditions are the factors which kept changing our routes very frequently. 

The original proposed plan was 
UK - France - Germany - Austria - Hungary - Romania - Bulgaria - Turkey - Georgia - Azerbaijan - Kazakhstan - Kyrgyzstan - China - Nepal - India

We found out that Nepal border is currently closed due to earthquake in 2015. And hence entering India from Myanmar is the next option.
Chinese travel agencies suggested avoiding Tibet and entering from Mongolian border  into China and then go south to Myanmar (this can reduce paper work and cost involved) but that means taking a complete de route to go to India and many more days on the road.

We contemplated the safety in Turkey and also predictability of crossing the Caspian Sea by the Cargo Ship. It involved more time at border crossing and shipping the boat. So we decided to go by Russia.

Considering the safety issue in Russia we decided to go from St. Petersburg down south to Sochi. Sochi being the host city for Olympics we expect better roads there and from Sochi enter in to Georgia, Azerbaijan, Turkmenistan, Kyrgyzstan.

Reading more on Georgia Russia relations and the controversies, passing by that border will increase the uncertainties. Also passing through Turkmenistan and  Azerbaijan involves more Visas.

So the final decided route  up to now is US- UK - France - Switzerland - Germany - Poland - Lithuania - Latvia - Estonia - Russia - Kazakhstan - Kyrgyzstan - China - Myanmar - India. 

I think route is very dynamic and keeps changing with more research. Hopeful to just reach Mumbai.
