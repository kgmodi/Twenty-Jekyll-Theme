Travelling through China



We contacted NAVO tours, Tibet tours  and few other travel agencies to inquire about the logistics of driving through China. The primary research showed that China and Tibet in specific needed elaborate paperwork for self driving trips in China.
The paperwork and requirements are as follows
1. Temporary Chinese driving license and vehicle number plate are required to drive a foreign registered vehicle in China.
2. The route should be fixed and approved by the government.
3. A Chinese guide must accompany the group throughout the trip.
4. Since 2007, the Chinese customs require vehicle deposit (depending on the value of car, manufacture year and displacement of the vehicle). The deposit is paid at the entry border/port and is refunded once the vehicle leaves China.

Entry in India can be from
1.China - Nepal - India
China - Nepal (Zhangmu Kodari) is closed post earthquake on 25th April 2015. Hence it is not possible to enter India from Nepal.
Another China - Nepal border (Guyrong) is currently open only to common tourist groups but not to self driving vehicles.
2. China - Sikkim(India)
Nathu La Pass border only opens to Indian pilgrim groups.
3. There is no established diplomatic relationship between China and Bhutan.
4. China - Myanmar - India
China - Myanmar (Ruili) Muse border is available at present and thus entering India from Moreh (Manipur - India).

Kunal, Shrida and I wanted to travel to Mt. Kailash when we are in China. Mount Kailash and lake Mansarovar has been of great significance while growing up.
We realized that to travel to Kailash needs a permit from National Tourism Admin of China as well as provincial Xinjiang and Tibet travel permit.
Tibet travel permit (issued for Tibetian Tourism Bureau) is complicated for Indian passport holders as they need a Special permit (from China Indian Pilgrim Service Center) to enter Mt. Kailash.
If  we intend to enter by Myanmar and pass through other parts of China Indian passport holders will also need a normal Tibet permit.
And Normal and Special Tibet permit both are not granted to the person at the same time.

Thus we realized
- Driving in China is elaborate paper work.
- It is expensive to have the guide and pay for the custom deposits.
- It is difficult to visit Mt. Kailash.

This gave us second thoughts on
- Entering India through Pakistan border
- To do a back pack trip and avoid the liability of the Car.
- To travel through China straight to Mongolia and then travel south. (Avoiding Tibet and thus the extra expenses).

After discussions and arguments we decided to stick to the Krygystan - China - Myanmar - India route. We decided to look for other travel groups and join with them and thus share the costs.
We are hoping for stars to work in our favor and China - Nepal border to open up by Aug 2016.
