Travelling through China



We contacted three travel agencies to inquire about the logistics of driving through China. NAVO tours was the one who replied to us promptly and gave us good information.Self driving vehicles needed the following permits and have the following requirements in China
1.   Paperwork, Temporary Chinese driving license and vehicle number plate.
Appointment of an entrusted travel agency is a must to obtain local travel permits (for Xinjiang and Tibet).To drive a foreign-registered vehicle in China, based on China traffic laws and regulations, temporary Chinese driver licenses and vehicle number plates are required
2.   Route & Itinerary
The travel route/itinerary should be fixed and approved by the government. During the trip, the vehicle should follow a fixed route.
     
3.    Tour guide
Based on the relevant regulations at present, a Chinese guide (who may speak Chinese, English/French/German) is a must to accompany the group during the journey in China. A spare seat has to be ensured for the guide in the car.
4.    Custom deposit
Since 2007, the Chinese customs requires vehicle deposit when foreign vehicles enter into China. Usually, the amount of the deposit depends on the manufacture year, the value and the displacement of the vehicle. Guests should pay the deposit at the customs of the entry border/port. On leaving China, the exit border/port will send a notice to the entry border/port, then the deposit will be full refunded.

Travel to Kailash
We figured that it was not possible for Indian passport holders to travel to Mount Kailash and Mansarovar lake.
The permits needed for this trip are
1. National approval - by the National Tourism Admin of China. Approval by this agency makes ones entry and exit in China legal.
2. Xinjiang travel permit and Tibet Travel permit- These are provincial permits necessary to enter these areas.
There are 2 kinds of Tibet travel permits  depending on nationality of individual.
Normal TTP(Tibet Travel permit) : It is issued by the Tibetan Tourism Bureau and is applicable to all citizens except Indian.If the tourists are not Indian passport holders, they can visit most areas of Tibet with this permit, including the Ali (Ngari) area, Mt. Kailash and Lake Manasarovar.
Indian passport holders are not allowed to access Ali (Ngari) area with this normal permit, they need another special permit. this special permit is issued by a special official agency CIPSCâ€”The China Indian Pilgrimsâ€™ Service Centre, this is a special permit for the Indian groups visiting  Ali(Ngari) area.
If Nepal border (Zhangmu) was currently open we would have been able to drive to Kailash and then to Nepal by obtaining special permit from CIPSC 
 But the problem faced at present is Nepal border is closed. After arriving at Manasarovar lake, to continue driving to other areas of Tibet (such as Shigatse, Lhasa, Nyingchi), then exit Tibet into Yunnan province  to the Myanmar border, the normal TTP will be required 
The current policies of Tibetan authority do not issue both permits to Indian tourists, so visiting both Ali (Ngari) Area and other areas of Tibet is not possible for India passport holders.

Thus we realized 
  • It is difficult to visit Kailash.
  • We need elaborate paper work.
  • Travelling through China is expensive.
