Travelling through China



We contacted three travel agencies to inquire about the logistics of driving through China. NAVO tours was the one who replied to us promptly and gave us good information.


Basic information about doing a self-driving trip in China:
1.   Paperwork, Temporary Chinese driving license and vehicle number plate.
For doing road trip in China, you need to entrust a qualified travel agency to deal with the necessary paperwork and get local travel permits(for Xinjiang and Tibet) from related Chinese government departments, normally, these procedures take around 2 months to be finished, so in order to get all procedures done in time, all the relevant documents have to be sent to agency at least 2 months before entering China.
To drive a foreign-registered vehicle in China, based on China traffic laws and regulations, temporary Chinese driver licenses and vehicle number plates are required, which the agency can help to obtain them.(Note : the age limit for car drivers in China is 18-69 years).

2.   Route & Itinerary
The travel route/itinerary should be fixed and approved by the government. During the trip, the vehicles/guests should follow the fixed route.
     
3.    Tour guide
Based on the relevant regulations at present, a Chinese guide (who may speak Chinese, English/French/Germany) is a must to accompany the group during the journey in China. So a spare seat needs to be ensured for the guide.

4.    Custom deposit
Since 2007, the Chinese customs requires vehicle deposit when foreign vehicles enter into China. Usually, the amount of the deposit depends on the manufacture year, the value and the displacement of the vehicle. Guests should pay the deposit at the customs of the entry border/port. On leaving China, the exit border/port will send a notice to the entry border/port, then the deposit will be full refunded. Agency can cover the deposit if entered from Kyrgyzstan.

Entering India

1.China - Nepal border:


China-Nepal border Zhangmu (Kodari) was closed post earthquake  on 25th April 2015. Hence it is not possible to enter India from Nepal at present.

Another China-Nepal border Guyrong had opened up few weeks ago, but so far, it only open to common tourist groups, foreign self-driving groups are not allowed to cross this border at present.

2.China-Sikkim border:
Nathu La Pass border(Sikkim border) only opens to India pilgrim groups which have to be organized by local authorities.

There’s no border between China and Bhutan as there are no established diplomatic relationship till now.

3. Myanmar-China border Ruili(Muse) or China-Laos border are the options available at present.

Travel to Kailash
It is not possible for Indian passport holders to visit Mt.Kailash and Manasarovar lake at present; to explain it, the permits needed for this trip are
1.National approval, your self-driving trip has to be approved by the National Tourism Admin of China, it makes your journey in China legally, you can only enter into and exit from China with this national approval.

2. After getting the national approval, provincial permits are required. Xinjiang travel permit and Tibet Travel permit, are issued by the local authorities, in China, these 2 provinces authorities require the foreign tourist to get local travel permit.

3. There are 2 kinds of Tibet travel permits  depending on nationality of individual.

Normal TTP(Tibet Travel permit) : It is issued by the Tibetan Tourism Bureau and is applicable to all citizens except Indian.
If the tourists are not Indian passport holders, they can visit most areas of Tibet with this permit, including the Ali (Ngari) area, Mt. Kailash and Lake Manasarovar.                                                                                                   Indian passport holders are not allowed to access Ali (Ngari) area with this normal permit, they need another special permit. this special permit is issued by a special official agency CIPSC—The China Indian Pilgrims’ Service Centre, this is a special permit for the Indian groups which is going to visit Ali(Ngari) area      ( Mt. Kailash or Lake Manasarovar).
'If Nepal border Zhangmu is still open, then you will be able to drive along our route NAVO-SD-102 ', stated NAVO tours; with the special permit which is issued by CIPSC.
 But the problem faced at present is Nepal border is closed. After arriving at Manasarovar lake, to continue driving to other areas of Tibet (such as Shigatse, Lhasa, Nyingchi), then exit Tibet into Yunnan province  to the Myanmar border, the normal TTP will be required 
The current policies of Tibetan authority do not issue both permits to Indian tourists, so visiting both Ali (Ngari) Area and other areas of Tibet is not possible for India passport holders.

Thus we realized 

  • It is difficult to visit Kailash.
  • We need elaborate paper work.
  • Travelling through China is expensive.
